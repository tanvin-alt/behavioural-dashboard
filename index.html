Designing for the 100th use

Imagine you are a B2B dashboard super user

What if the interface remembered how you worked and reorganised itself around it? This demo compresses weeks of real usage into seconds of interaction.

This works best for single-user, repeated workflows in B2B tools: logistics ops, support desks, trading terminals. Shared devices need session-level identity or the layout learns a muddled average of everyone. It only tracks clicks too, not attention. Someone reading a panel for two minutes registers nothing. Any system like this should ask before it acts, not surprise you with a rearranged workspace. That is why the suggestion here waits for your confirmation. These are not blockers. They are the next design problems :)
